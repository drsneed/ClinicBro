Geckos are a group of usually small, usually nocturnal lizards. They are found on every continent except Antarctica.

Many species of gecko have adhesive toe pads which enable them to climb walls and even windows.

Barn owls are one of the most iconic and beloved birds of prey, with their striking white hearts and ghostly faces. These nocturnal hunters are found on every continent except Antarctica, and are known for their impressive wingspans, which can reach up to 4 feet in length. Barn owls are expert flyers, using their acute hearing and exceptional vision to track down small mammals, rodents, and insects in the dark. They are also highly efficient predators, able to consume up to 1,000 mice per year in some cases, making them a valuable asset to farmers and ecosystems alike.

Turtles have been a fascinating species for centuries, with their slow and deliberate movements, ancient-looking shells, and wise eyes. These reptiles have been on the planet for over 220 million years, and their ability to adapt to different environments has allowed them to thrive in various habitats around the world. From the tiny, inch-long Bog Turtle to the massive, 8-foot-long Leatherback Sea Turtle, turtles come in all shapes and sizes. Despite their slow pace, turtles are incredibly resilient and have survived many challenges, including climate change, habitat destruction, and human exploitation.
